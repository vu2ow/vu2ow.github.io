Bad radios

A microwave oven cooks the food by passing high power (about 750 - 1000) watts of small wavelength, high frequency (2000 Mega Hertz MHz) radio waves. This radiation happens inside a shielded chamber so that it doesn’t come out. There are folks who tried to beat the mechanism (door open warning) and have gone blind by peering into the cooking chamber. Facts and studies on damage caused to the food by such radiation are like several cans of worms. Radiation from cell phones and cell phone towers heat up body tissue. Also, there is a claim that this radiation caused DNA damage and cause cancer. Higher the frequency, shorter is the wavelength and higher is the absorption rate in the body! So, all radio waves are harmful but higher frequencies are deadly. In olden days, they used to treat body aches with high power radio waves directed on the body. They used longer wavelengths…works by heating up the affected area… but this treatment has been quietly banned now! There is a case where a person accidentally walked across a big C band (5000 MHz) dish antenna on roof top transmitting TV program to satellite and died few days later because of exposure.

We are relentlessly bombarded by many type of electromagnetic radiations in our life. FCC of US regulates that any appliance should not emit harmful radiation which will interfere with other devices. But with our muniyandi brand mixies and other devices, who is enforcing these regulations?

Ham radio operators, who are crazy about their hobby are constantly worried about interference in their spectrum. Those days are gone when the “band was quiet” so that one could hear transmission from a fellow ham operating thousands of miles of away easily.

Ham radio operators report signals from other stations with RST system.

R -  Readability - Understanding what is said and how well. On a scale of 1 to 5, the readability of your signal with a "5" being perfect with no difficulty. In other words the ability of the other operator to understand what you are saying. A "1" is unreadable....a "5" is perfectly readable.

S -  Strength - On a scale of 1 to 9, indicates how strong your stations signal is. A "1" is a very faint signal. A"9" is an extremely strong signal.


T - Used for morse code signal reports. Indicates on a scale of 1 to 9 the quality of the tone of the morse code "dits and dahs".  From a "60 cycle harsh tone" a (1).... To a "very pure tone", a (9).

A good sounding phone (voice) signal from a fellow ham reported as 59 means readability is perfect (5) and strength is extremely strong (9). These days, due to the noise from “radio pollution “, my radio in Chennai reads 59 without any transmission at all! It means that it is receiving noise at that signal level! It is very difficult for me to work with other stations with signal strength less than 57! The other station has to break the noise floor! Only solace is that with improvements in digital signal processing and growth in chip technology it is possible to filter out signal from noise better. However, an average enthusiastic “home brewer” is finding it more and more difficult to source components (companies would not distribute parts… and even if they do, special labs are needed to solder them without damage) and build radios.

Ham folks take pride in designing their own antennas and build their own radios to talk to each other from great distances with minimal power (sometimes as low as 1 Watt to 100 watts) and less bandwidth. There is a breed of hams that will not transmit more than 5 watts – these are called QRP enthusiasts! The bands have become so noisy these days in the cities with radio pollution from appliances, automobiles, you name them. Often ham radio folks move away from the city for a day or two, on “field days”… take their radios on boats, mountain tops and have field day.
